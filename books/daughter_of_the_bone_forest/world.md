# World & Magic

## Magic System
{: #magic-system }

Four types of magic, each tied to a geographical source and an "anchor" animal:

| Type | Source | Anchor | Powers |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Bone** | [Bone Forest](#bone-forest) | Wolves | Necromancy, communion with the dead, bone armor on shifted forms |
| **Ice** | [Frozen Mountain](#frozen-mountain) | Ravens | Prophecy, dream interpretation, frost abilities |
| **Glass** | [Obsidian Desert](#obsidian-desert) | Pythons | Enchantment runes, protective charms |
| **Flower** | [Lake Bloom](#lake-bloom) | Whales (none in the kingdom) | Healing, potions, nature magic |

Each animal is ranked by how much magic it channels. A familiar's first shift indicates their raw power level. Bone wolves and ice ravens are the most powerful familiars in their respective types.

## Key Terminology

### Bone Familiar / Ice Familiar / Flower Familiar / Glass Familiar
{: #familiars }

Shifters aligned with one of four magic types. Each type has a geographical source and associated animals.

### Feral
{: #feral }

When a familiar's animal mind overwhelms their human mind, losing rational control. Can be temporary (stress-induced) or chronic (like [Gran](characters.md#gran)'s condition).

### Bone Rage
{: #bone-rage }

When a bone animal's eyes go white and it attacks everything indiscriminately. Different from going feral.

### Specter
{: #specter }

A monstrous undead entity formed from the fusion of multiple spirits. Far more dangerous than individual ghosts or spirits. Can teleport, phase between solid and ghostly states, and emit a howl that traps nearby ghosts.

### Quick Shift
{: #quick-shift }

Shifting directly from one animal form to another without reverting to human first. An advanced technique that most familiars cannot do.

### Bonding
{: #bonding }

The formal magical partnership between a witch and familiar. Strengthens both parties' powers. Strongest when touching. Forcing a bond is considered deeply immoral.

### Courtship
{: #courtship }

A formalized ritual where a witch presents a necklace imbued with their magical energy to a familiar. The familiar wears it for a week to test compatibility. The courter cannot speak to the courted during this period.

### Page Duty
{: #page-duty }

A punishment assignment at assemblies. The enchanted tabard forces the wearer to obey commands from anyone present.

### Feats
{: #feats }

Competitive events at school assemblies (not called "games"). Military recruiters attend to scout talent.

### Thane
{: #thane }

A military/noble rank. [Shaw](characters.md#shaw)'s entourage members are guaranteed this rank.

### Jarls' Council
{: #jarls-council }

The governing body of regional lords. Graduates of [Witch Hall](#witch-hall) can petition them.

### Iktus
{: #iktus }

The lowest social class in the kingdom.

### Samhain
{: #samhain }

The bone-magic seasonal holiday, when bone witches perform rituals to commune with the dead. Strongest in the Bone Forest.

### Lammas / Candlemas / Beltane
{: #seasonal-holidays }

The other seasonal holidays for glass, ice, and flower magic respectively, each tied to their geographical source.

## Locations

### The Cursed Kingdom
{: #cursed-kingdom }

The nation where the story takes place. One of the few places where witches and familiars are safe from being burned.

### Witch Hall
{: #witch-hall }

The school for witches and familiars. Founded by the Witch Queen. Requires a senior project and faculty advisor to graduate.

### Bone Forest
{: #bone-forest }

Sentient magical woodland made of bone pines -- trees with exposed bone-like bark that can move on their own. Has a special bond with [Rosy](characters.md#rosy). Source of bone magic.

### Frozen Mountain
{: #frozen-mountain }

Sentient/magical mountain where the Witch King resides and [Shaw](characters.md#shaw) grew up. Source of ice magic.

### Lake Bloom
{: #lake-bloom }

Source of flower magic, located near [Multah](#multah). Has an underwater magical ecosystem with sentient flowers and dangerous creatures.

### Obsidian Desert
{: #obsidian-desert }

Source of glass magic. Home to obsidian miners like [Oluk](characters.md#oluk)'s family.

### Forest's Edge
{: #forests-edge }

[Rosy](characters.md#rosy)'s hometown, a small settlement at the border of the Bone Forest.

### Multah
{: #multah }

A major town in the Cursed Kingdom, near Lake Bloom. Site of the terrorist fire.

### Vinland
{: #vinland }

An anti-magic empire across the [Wimahl River](#wimahl-river). The antagonist nation that is moving toward war with the Cursed Kingdom.

### Vingate
{: #vingate }

Vinland's main western port, across from Multah.

### Wimahl River
{: #wimahl-river }

Border between the Cursed Kingdom and Vinland.

### Daming
{: #daming }

A great nation across the ocean, home country of [Madam Xu](characters.md#madam-xu), Madam Bai, and Mister Xu.

### Waiming Territories
{: #waiming }

A region where many Daming refugees settled. [Yuyan](characters.md#yuyan) and [Guanyu](characters.md#guanyu)'s families are from here.

## Running Gags

**"Rosy, Rosy"** -- Nearly every character addresses Rosy by repeating her name twice when trying to get her attention or calm her down. Gran, Shantie, Guanyu, Toketie, and even the Bone Forest itself all do this.

**Shaw's mare "Cow"** -- The princess's bone horse is named Cow. Characters react to this absurd name repeatedly. When Cow charges the specter to defend Shaw, Shaw gasps "Cow?" in disbelief.

**Rosy's stomach/food obsession** -- Rosy is perpetually hungry and thinks about food at the worst times. She arrives at Witch Hall focused on breakfast, gets distracted by crises but keeps circling back to meals.

**Charles being the worst** -- Charles Almstedt's escalating pettiness becomes almost comedic: stealing Rosy's rabbit during an exam by shifting into a bobcat, pushing her into dirty dish water, commanding her to kneel during page duty, setting horses loose to frame her.

**The Bone Forest as a needy child** -- The Forest acts like a toddler: tugging at Rosy's hair and skirt, refusing to let her go where she wants, blocking paths to force her toward things it thinks are important, sulking when ignored. It literally rearranges trees to manipulate her.

**"I'm here"** -- Rosy's simple declaration of loyalty. She says it to the Bone Forest in Chapter 1 and to Shaw in the climax. It bookends the story.

**Oluk's dream witch** -- Oluk describes his ideal future partner in romantic detail (respectful, equal partnership). Einar turns out to match the description perfectly.

**Shaw's mask/control** -- Characters repeatedly try to crack Shaw's emotionless facade. Rosy feels "satisfied to have broken the princess's careful control" when Shaw laughs.
